## The Real Problem

<p class="slide-lead">Costings are too inaccurate and time-consuming to produce. They often lack inputs from other providers and do not reliably reflect local and market conditions.</p>

<div class="proposal-grid three-up tha-problem-grid">
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Cycle Time</p>
    <h3>Costing takes too long</h3>
    <p>Manual collection and external dependencies slow down early-stage estimates.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Completeness</p>
    <h3>Outputs arrive incomplete</h3>
    <p>Missing provider inputs and market assumptions weaken early decisions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Reuse</p>
    <h3>Knowledge is fragmented</h3>
    <p>Local project knowledge exists, but it is hard to retrieve and apply consistently.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Solution Shape

## Why XYZ

<p class="slide-lead">This is a boutique data problem: the hard part is understanding which data can be trusted, how estimates should be traced, and where automation helps with review built in.</p>

<div class="tha-why-layout">
  <div class="tha-why-thesis">
    <span class="metric-label">Why this needs a careful method</span>
    <strong>Generic AI delivery is too optimistic for commercially sensitive costing work.</strong>
    <p>A phased process exposes risk early, keeps expert judgement in control, and only scales once THA trusts the data and the workflow.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="tha-why-points">
    <div><span>01</span><p>Costing logic is domain-specific and tied to THA accountability.</p></div>
    <div><span>02</span><p>Source quality varies across documents, systems, paper, and third-party inputs.</p></div>
    <div><span>03</span><p>The right path is discovery, representative test data, and controlled validation stages.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

## Delivery Model

<div class="timeline-grid tha-phase-grid">
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">01</span><h3>Startup</h3><p>Discovery workshop, job shadowing, data audit, scope, risks, and success criteria.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">02</span><h3>De-risking</h3><p>Vertical slice on limited test data to validate ingestion, mapping, traceability, and review gates.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">03</span><h3>Prototype</h3><p>First testable internal solution around the highest-value use case.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">04</span><h3>Pilot</h3><p>Calibration against real or near-real costing examples and expert review.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">05</span><h3>Rollout</h3><p>Broader workflow integration, user feedback, and operating model refinement.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline-card"><span class="metric-label">06</span><h3>Adoption</h3><p>Stress tests, edge cases, QA process, audit trail, permissions, and training.</p></div>
</div>

## What Each Phase Proves

<div class="proposal-grid two-up tha-proof-grid">
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Startup</p>
    <h3>Scope the right workflow</h3>
    <p>Discovery workshop, job shadowing, data-source audit, gap analysis, priority use cases, success criteria, and risk analysis.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">De-risking</p>
    <h3>Test real feasibility</h3>
    <p>Limited representative data, ingestion and extraction tests, mapping, estimate ranges, source traceability, labour-cost assumptions, and review points.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Prototype &amp; Pilot</p>
    <h3>Validate usefulness</h3>
    <p>A first internal solution, demos and workshops, extended knowledge base, calibration against real examples, and quality thresholds.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="proposal-card">
    <p class="eyebrow text-accent">Rollout &amp; Adoption</p>
    <h3>Make it operational</h3>
    <p>Workflow integration, user feedback, stress tests, edge cases, QA process, audit trail, permissions, and team training.</p>
  </div>
</div>
